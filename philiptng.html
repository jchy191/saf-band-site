OUR PEOPLE

SENIOR DIRECTOR OF MUSIC

ME6 Philip Tng Liat Peng

SAF BAND

Having started his musical education on the piano at the age of ten, ME6 Philip Tng has since rose through tremendous musical milestones to reach his current position as the Senior Director of Music of the Singapore Armed Forces (SAF) Band.

As a saxophonist with the SAF Music and Drama Company, he was awarded a scholarship to the Royal Military School of Music, Kneller Hall in London. ME6 Tng graduated in 1993 from the Bandmaster Course with several accolades including the first prize in the Fanfare Award of the Fred Mortimer Memorial Competition, second prize in the Besson Cup and Medal, the Sommerville Prize, as well as the Graham Wallace Award for being the best overseas student bandmaster. Subsequently, ME6 Tng went on to become the Director of Music of the SAF Central Band. He held this position for five years before assuming the post of Senior Director of Music of the SAF Band in August 2000.

In 2008, ME6 Tng received another scholarship from the Singapore Armed Forces to pursue his Masters in Wind Band Conducting at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. During his studies, he had the privilege to study conducting with numerous renowned conductors, namely, James Keene, Abel Ramirez, Roby George, Robert W. Rumbelow, Tim Foley, Stephen Peterson and Russel Mikkelson. Graduating with excellent academic achievements, he was initiated into the Phi Kappa Phi Chapter at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

ME6 Tng is active in many musical activities in Singapore, including conducting the National Day Parade and the adjudication of local music competitions and festivals, such as the Singapore Youth Festival and the Singapore International Band Festival. In 2005, both the SAF Central Band and his community band, West Winds, were selected to perform at the World Association of Symphonic Bands and Ensembles (WASBE) Conference held in Singapore. ME6 Tng has also been invited as guest conductor and adjudicator in several countries including Australia, Hong Kong, Malaysia and the United States of America. In addition to his duties in the SAF, he also serves as a Board Member of the Wind Band Association of Singapore Executive Committee.

Apart from his musical studies, ME6 Tng was the first Director of Music to attend the Command and Staff (Executive) Course at the Goh Keng Swee Command and Staff College in 2013, where he was awarded the Book Prize for coming in top of his graduating cohort in Security Studies.
